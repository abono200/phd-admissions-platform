# PhD Admissions Dossier: Massachusetts Institute of Technology

## 1. University Overview
* **QS World University Ranking (2026/2027):** #1
* **CSrankings.org Ranking:** #6
* **Target Department:** Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science (EECS)
* **Research Reputation:** The premier technological university in the world, leading in ML, healthcare AI, and computing systems.
* **Key Research Laboratories:** Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CSAIL), Abdul Latif Jameel Clinic for Machine Learning in Health (Jameel Clinic)
* **Core Strengths:** Health Informatics, Machine Learning, Artificial Intelligence, Computational Policy.

---

## 2. Funding Analysis
* **Funding Guaranteed:** Yes, 100% guaranteed for the duration of the PhD
* **Tuition Waiver:** 100% covered
* **Annual Stipend Range:** $51,226
* **Monthly Stipend Range:** $4,269
* **Health Insurance Coverage:** 100% covered (MIT Student Health Insurance Plan)
* **Assistantship Dynamics:** Research Assistantships (RA) and Teaching Assistantships (TA) are standard. Most students transition to RAs full-time after passing quals.
* **Other Support:** Relocation assistance, computing resources, and travel funding for major conferences.
* **Summer Funding:** Typically covered via summer Research Assistantships (included in the annual range above).
* **Funding Duration:** 5 Years (guaranteed subject to satisfactory academic progress).
* **Renewal Requirements:** Maintain a minimum GPA (usually 3.0), complete required teaching assignments (usually 1-2 semesters), and show continuous progress on thesis research.

---

## 3. Admissions Requirements
* **Minimum GPA Expectations:** 3.8+ (First Class is expected)
* **Preferred Academic Background:** Excellent academic background in CS, physics, mathematics, or engineering.
* **Master's Degree Required:** No (Direct admission from BSc is possible, but your MPhil is a massive advantage).
* **Programming Skills Expected:** Python, C/C++, R, or similar language. Ability to build ML/DL pipelines (PyTorch, TensorFlow) and manage databases (SQL, Pandas).
* **Mathematics Background:** Strong linear algebra, calculus, probability, and statistics.
* **Research Experience:** Highly preferred. Evidence of independent research (such as a thesis) is critical.
* **Publication Expectations:** Highly valued. At least one co-authored paper in a peer-reviewed venue or workshop is preferred.
* **English Language Requirements & Waiver:** EECS is very strict. They officially require IELTS or TOEFL for non-native speakers. However, you can request an exception or submit a Duolingo test if TOEFL is unavailable.
* **GRE Requirements:** Not Accepted (will not be reviewed)
* **Application Fee:** $75
* **Fee Waiver Availability:** Available. Apply directly through the EECS portal under the diversity/financial need guidelines.

## 6. Step-by-Step Admission Strategy
1. **Identify Projects:** Read 2-3 recent papers by David Sontag and David Sontag. Note down how their methodologies align with your past medical database knowledge discovery or social media parsing.
2. **Draft Supervisor Email:** Reach out in October. Keep it under 250 words, attach your CV, cite their recent paper, and highlight your MPhil and first-class status.
3. **Secure Reference Letters:** Ask academic advisors who can vouch for your programming and research capabilities.
4. **Draft Statement of Purpose (SOP):** Structure it logically: 25% past background/MPhil research, 25% your publication achievements, 25% why Massachusetts Institute of Technology is the ideal fit, and 25% your future research vision under the matched faculty.
